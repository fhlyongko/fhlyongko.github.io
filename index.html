About

Yong-Jik Lee is a Professor in the College of Sarim Honors at Changwon National University, where his teaching and research focus on the intersection of artificial intelligence and education.

His scholarship investigates how AI is reshaping higher education and language learning — spanning AI literacy across academic disciplines, the integration of generative AI into English language teaching, English-Medium Instruction (EMI), and the cognitive foundations of multimedia learning. Drawing on both survey and experimental designs, his work asks how students and pre-service teachers come to understand, adopt, and learn effectively with emerging technologies, and what universities must do to support responsible and pedagogically meaningful AI use.

In 2026 he has published a sustained body of work in Scopus- and SSCI-indexed international journals, often co-authored with colleagues across Korean and Chinese universities, and was selected for the National Research Foundation of Korea (NRF) Early-Career Researcher Program. He is also active as an invited speaker on academic English writing and AI literacy at Korean universities.

Research Areas

AI Literacy in Higher Education

How university students and pre-service teachers develop knowledge, attitudes, and competencies around AI — examined across academic disciplines, year levels, and majors, including international and trade-focused student populations.

Generative AI in Language Learning

The use, perception, and pedagogical value of generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, machine translation) in English as a Foreign Language settings, and the AI literacy instruction needed to support them.

English-Medium Instruction & Academic Communication

Cross-cultural and gendered patterns in student–faculty communication within EMI contexts, with attention to pragmatics, formality, and request strategies in academic correspondence.

Multimedia Learning & Cognitive Load

Experimental study of how working memory and contextual features such as elaborative and seductive details shape interest, cognitive load, and learning from educational video.

    Davis, R. O., Lee, Y.-J., Vincent, J., & Lee, J. H.

    Sung, S.-e., Davis, R. O., Vincent, J., & Lee, Y.-J.
    Education Sciences (MDPI), 16, 2026Scopus
    Analysis of 113 advanced learners' English emails to Korean vs. international professors, revealing how culture and self-reported gender shape formality and request strategies in EMI settings.

    Lee, Y.-J., Davis, R. O., & Choi, J.
    The Journal of Asia TEFL, 23(1), 128–137, 2026 · doi.org/10.18823/asiatefl.2026.23.1.10.128Scopus
    A survey of 73 undergraduates on their use and perception of generative AI (ChatGPT, Google Translate) in English learning, highlighting the need for structured AI literacy instruction.

    Lee, Y.-J., & Choi, H.-C.
    International Journal of Information and Education Technology, 16(6), 2026Scopus
    A pre/post-semester study of 108 students applying the Technology Acceptance Model to explain how academic discipline shapes AI acceptance and use.
